The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is Indiaâ€™s national space agency, established on August 15, 1969 by Dr. Vikram Sarabhai. Headquartered in Bengaluru, ISRO focuses on using space technology for national development and scientific exploration. Key achievements include the launch of Aryabhata (India's first satellite), Chandrayaan-1 (discovered water on the Moon), Mangalyaan (Mars mission), and Chandrayaan-3 (first successful Moon south pole landing). It has developed reliable launch vehicles like PSLV and GSLV, and launched Aditya-L1 (solar mission). ISRO is also preparing for its first human spaceflight mission, Gaganyaan.
